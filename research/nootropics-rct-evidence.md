# Nootropics: RCT Evidence Summary
Generated: 2026-04-05
Sources: Examine.com (live), PubMed, knowledge base (cutoff Aug 2025)

---

## Ranking criteria
Compounds below were selected for having 2+ human RCTs directly measuring cognitive outcomes.
Evidence quality ratings follow Examine.com grades where available (A=strong replication, B=good
evidence, C=moderate, D=preliminary/conflicting) and are cross-checked against PubMed meta-analyses.

---

## 1. Creatine Monohydrate

- **Primary benefit:** Working memory, processing speed (especially under cognitive load or sleep deprivation)
- **Evidence quality:** Strong RCT (Examine Grade B-A for cognition; 20+ human trials)
- **Key RCT evidence:** A 2003 double-blind crossover RCT (Rae et al., *Psychopharmacology*, n=45
  vegetarians) found 5g/day for 6 weeks significantly improved working memory (Backward Digit Span)
  and intelligence test scores vs placebo. A 2007 RCT (McMorris et al.) showed 20g/day for 7 days
  attenuated cognitive decline during sleep deprivation. Meta-analyses confirm benefits are most
  pronounced in populations with lower baseline creatine (vegetarians, elderly, sleep-deprived).
- **Sources:**
  - https://examine.com/supplements/creatine/
  - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/14561278/ (Rae et al. 2003)
  - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/17828627/ (McMorris et al. 2007)
- **Notes:** Effect size modest in well-nourished omnivores. Long-term neuroprotective potential
  (mitochondrial support) is promising but less established by RCT. Typical cognitive dose: 3–5g/day.

## 2. Bacopa Monnieri

- **Primary benefit:** Memory consolidation, delayed recall; secondary anxiety reduction
- **Evidence quality:** Strong RCT (Examine: 25 studies, 516+ participants for memory outcome)
- **Key RCT evidence:** A 2001 double-blind RCT (Roodenrys et al., *Neuropsychopharmacology*, n=76,
  adults 40–65) showed 300mg/day for 12 weeks significantly improved delayed word recall vs placebo.
  A 2008 RCT (Stough et al., n=62) replicated memory improvement at 300mg/day over 90 days, with
  strongest effects on delayed recall and rate of learning. Effects require 8–12 weeks to manifest.
- **Sources:**
  - https://examine.com/supplements/bacopa-monnieri/
  - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/11498727/ (Roodenrys et al. 2001)
  - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18683852/ (Stough et al. 2008)
- **Notes:** Consistent across multiple labs and populations. Mechanism: bacosides enhance synaptic
  transmission and reduce oxidative stress in hippocampus. GI side effects at higher doses. The
  memory benefit is specifically for *delayed* recall, not immediate or working memory.

## 3. Rhodiola Rosea

- **Primary benefit:** Mental fatigue resistance, stress resilience, sustained attention under load
- **Evidence quality:** Strong RCT (Examine: Grade A for cognition; 22 trials, 1,944 participants,
  2 meta-analyses)
- **Key RCT evidence:** A 2000 RCT (Shevtsov et al., *Phytomedicine*, n=161 military cadets) found
  a single dose of rhodiola extract significantly improved mental performance, fatigue, and
  neuro-motor tests vs placebo. A 2009 RCT (Olsson et al., *Planta Medica*, n=60 with stress-related
  fatigue) showed 576mg/day for 28 days significantly improved burnout symptoms and cognitive function.
  A 2015 head-to-head RCT vs sertraline for depression (Mao et al.) showed comparable efficacy with
  fewer side effects.
- **Sources:**
  - https://examine.com/supplements/rhodiola-rosea/
  - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/19016404/ (Olsson et al. 2009)
  - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/10761538/ (Shevtsov et al. 2000)
- **Notes:** Adaptogen effects are most reproducible in stressed/fatigued populations. Examine lists
  Mental Resilience as its primary application. Typical dose: 200–600mg/day of 3% rosavins extract.

## 4. Caffeine + L-Theanine (Stack)

- **Primary benefit:** Focused attention, processing speed, reduced caffeine jitteriness
- **Evidence quality:** Strong RCT (Examine: 20 studies on theanine for cognition; stack has
  dedicated RCTs)
- **Key RCT evidence:** A landmark 2008 double-blind crossover RCT (Owen et al., *Biological
  Psychology*, n=27) found the 100mg caffeine + 50mg L-theanine combination significantly improved
  speed and accuracy on attention-switching and numeric working memory tasks compared to either
  compound alone or placebo. A 2010 RCT (Haskell et al., n=24) replicated improved accuracy and
  alert/calm mood ratings with the same dose. Effects onset within 30–60 minutes.
- **Sources:**
  - https://examine.com/supplements/theanine/
  - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18006208/ (Owen et al. 2008)
  - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18554900/ (Haskell et al. 2008)
- **Notes:** Most consistently studied nootropic combination. L-theanine alone has weaker evidence
  for healthy non-sleep-deprived individuals; the synergy with caffeine is well-replicated. Examine
  grades theanine C for anxiety reduction in isolation. Dose ratio 2:1 (caffeine:theanine) is typical.

## 5. Omega-3 Fatty Acids (DHA/EPA)

- **Primary benefit:** Neuroprotection, slowing cognitive decline, mood support; DHA is structural
  component of neuronal membranes
- **Evidence quality:** Moderate-to-Strong RCT for decline prevention; mixed for acute cognition
  in healthy adults (Examine: 62 conditions covered, strong for triglycerides and depression)
- **Key RCT evidence:** A 2010 RCT (Dangour et al., *American Journal of Clinical Nutrition*, n=867
  adults 70–79) found no cognitive benefit of 500mg DHA+EPA/day over 24 months in healthy elderly —
  suggesting prevention-window matters. However, the MIDAS trial (Yurko-Mauro et al. 2010, n=485,
  adults with age-related cognitive decline) found 900mg/day DHA for 24 weeks significantly improved
  learning and memory vs placebo. A 2012 meta-analysis (Mazereeuw et al.) covering 10 RCTs found
  modest benefits for episodic memory in older adults.
- **Sources:**
  - https://examine.com/supplements/fish-oil/
  - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20861171/ (Yurko-Mauro et al. 2010 - MIDAS trial)
  - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/20861171/
- **Notes:** Evidence strongest for: (1) people with low baseline omega-3 intake, (2) age-related
  cognitive decline, (3) depression comorbidity. Healthy young adults with adequate dietary intake
  show minimal acute cognitive benefit. DHA-focused supplements preferred for brain outcomes.
  Typical dose: 1–3g/day combined EPA+DHA.

## 6. Phosphatidylserine (PS)

- **Primary benefit:** Age-related memory decline, attention in ADHD, cortisol/stress modulation
- **Evidence quality:** Moderate RCT (Examine: 13 trials, 856 participants; Grade B for cognitive
  decline in Alzheimer's patients, C for general cognitive improvement)
- **Key RCT evidence:** A key 1991 multicenter RCT (Crook et al., *Neurology*, n=149, age-related
  cognitive impairment) found 300mg/day bovine-PS for 12 weeks improved memory and learning tasks
  vs placebo; responders who were less impaired at baseline showed the largest gains. A 2010 RCT
  in children with ADHD (Hirayama et al., n=36) found 200mg/day soy-derived PS for 2 months
  improved attention and short-term auditory memory vs placebo.
- **Sources:**
  - https://examine.com/supplements/phosphatidylserine/
  - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/1944900/ (Crook et al. 1991)
- **Notes:** Early high-quality trials used bovine-derived PS (no longer commercially available);
  modern soy-derived PS shows smaller effects, which weakens the overall evidence picture. FDA allows
  a qualified health claim for PS and reduced dementia risk. Standard dose: 300mg/day in 3 splits.

## 7. Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera)

- **Primary benefit:** Stress/cortisol reduction, secondary cognitive improvement via stress pathway;
  sleep quality, reaction time
- **Evidence quality:** Strong RCT for stress/cortisol; Moderate RCT for direct cognition
  (Examine: dozens of studies, well-documented for stress outcomes)
- **Key RCT evidence:** A 2012 double-blind RCT (Chandrasekhar et al., *Indian Journal of
  Psychological Medicine*, n=64) found 300mg KSM-66 extract twice daily for 60 days reduced
  cortisol by 27.9% and significantly improved stress scores vs placebo. A 2017 RCT (Choudhary
  et al., *Journal of Dietary Supplements*, n=50) found 300mg/day for 8 weeks improved immediate
  and general memory, executive function, and processing speed on the Wechsler Memory Scale vs
  placebo in healthy adults.
- **Sources:**
  - https://examine.com/supplements/ashwagandha/
  - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/23439798/ (Chandrasekhar et al. 2012)
  - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/28471731/ (Choudhary et al. 2017)
- **Notes:** Cognitive benefits in healthy adults may be partially mediated through stress/cortisol
  reduction rather than direct nootropic action. Withanolide content varies widely between products —
  KSM-66 and Sensoril are the most studied standardized extracts. Typical dose: 300–600mg/day.

## 8. Citicoline (CDP-Choline)

- **Primary benefit:** Attention, memory support, neuroprotection; precursor for acetylcholine and
  uridine
- **Evidence quality:** Moderate RCT (Examine: 7 conditions covered; Grade B for some outcomes;
  strongest evidence in cognitively impaired populations)
- **Key RCT evidence:** A 1996 meta-analysis covering 13 RCTs (n=1,051, older adults with cognitive
  impairment) found CDP-choline produced significant improvements in memory and behavior. In healthy
  adults, a 2015 RCT (McGlade et al., *Food and Nutrition Sciences*, n=75 women 40–60) found 250mg
  or 500mg/day for 28 days improved attention, psychomotor speed, and working memory vs placebo,
  with 250mg outperforming 500mg on some measures. A 2012 RCT (Nakazaki et al.) found improvements
  in verbal memory in elderly subjects.
- **Sources:**
  - https://examine.com/supplements/citicoline/
  - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/26179181/ (McGlade et al. 2015)
- **Notes:** Citicoline is the better-studied choline source for cognition vs Alpha-GPC (see below),
  though both increase brain acetylcholine. Dose: 250–500mg/day. Well-tolerated with minimal side
  effects. Sometimes combined with racetams in research settings.

## 9. Alpha-GPC (Alpha-glycerophosphocholine)

- **Primary benefit:** Memory, Alzheimer's symptom attenuation, acute cognitive performance
- **Evidence quality:** Moderate RCT (Examine: Grade C for Alzheimer's symptom improvement with
  moderate effect; Grade B for growth hormone; 9 studies for dementia outcomes)
- **Key RCT evidence:** A multi-center Italian RCT (De Jesus Moreno Moreno 2003, *Clinical
  Therapeutics*, n=261 Alzheimer's patients) found 400mg three times daily for 180 days produced
  significant improvements on the ADAS-Cog and GCS scales vs placebo. In healthy adults, a 2015
  pilot RCT (Bellar et al.) found 600mg Alpha-GPC before exercise improved peak bench press force,
  suggesting cholinergic mechanisms affecting motor output. Direct cognitive RCTs in healthy adults
  are fewer than for citicoline.
- **Sources:**
  - https://examine.com/supplements/alpha-gpc/
  - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/12637119/ (De Jesus Moreno Moreno 2003)
- **Notes:** Strongest evidence in cognitively impaired populations. For healthy adults, stacking
  with racetams or other cholinergics is common in practice but less studied. May increase TMAO
  (a cardiovascular risk marker) at high doses — emerging concern as of 2021–2023. Dose: 300–600mg/day.

## 10. Lion's Mane Mushroom (Hericium erinaceus)

- **Primary benefit:** Nerve growth factor (NGF) stimulation, mild cognitive improvement in elderly,
  potential neuroregeneration
- **Evidence quality:** Mixed RCT (2–4 human RCTs; sample sizes small; results promising but need
  replication)
- **Key RCT evidence:** The foundational RCT (Mori et al. 2009, *Phytotherapy Research*, n=30 adults
  50–80 with mild cognitive impairment) found 1,000mg three times daily (3g/day) for 16 weeks
  significantly improved Hasegawa Dementia Scale scores vs placebo; scores declined after
  discontinuation. A 2020 parallel-group RCT (Saitsu et al., n=31 healthy adults 50–80) found
  improvements in Mini-Mental State scores. A 2023 RCT (Docherty et al., *Nutritional Neuroscience*,
  n=41 healthy young adults 18–45) found a single dose improved processing speed on the Stroop task
  vs placebo — the first acute-dose cognitive RCT.
- **Sources:**
  - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/18844328/ (Mori et al. 2009)
  - https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/37605458/ (Docherty et al. 2023)
- **Notes:** Evidence base is growing but still limited compared to Bacopa or Rhodiola. The Mori
  2009 study is well-cited but n=30 and single-center. Mechanistically compelling (hericenones/
  erinacines stimulate NGF synthesis). Typical dose: 500mg–3g/day of fruiting body extract;
  look for products specifying hericenone/erinacine content.

## Notable exclusions and why

- **Panax Ginseng:** 17 RCTs for cognitive outcomes on Examine but effect sizes are inconsistent
  and quality is variable; some positive findings for sustained attention and working memory but
  replication is poor across labs. Worth monitoring — not in top 10 by current evidence strength.
- **Vinpocetine:** Some RCTs but primarily in Eastern European populations with methodological
  concerns; regulatory status variable.
- **Racetams (Piracetam, Aniracetam):** Older RCT evidence, most in impaired populations; minimal
  quality modern RCTs in healthy adults. Not food/supplement in US.

---

## Key caveats

1. Most studies showing cognitive benefit are in: elderly adults, people with MCI, vegetarians
   (creatine), or stressed/sleep-deprived individuals. Healthy young adults with good diet and sleep
   show smaller effect sizes across the board.
2. "Strong RCT" here means 5+ replication studies with consistent direction, not that individual
   effect sizes are large. Most nootropic effect sizes are small-moderate (Cohen's d 0.3–0.6).
3. Supplement quality matters enormously. Bacopa, Rhodiola, Ashwagandha, and Lion's Mane all have
   significant variability in standardized extract content across brands.
4. No compound on this list has been shown in RCTs to produce permanent structural cognitive
   enhancement in healthy young adults — effects are typically acute (caffeine+theanine) or
   require continued supplementation (Bacopa, Creatine).
