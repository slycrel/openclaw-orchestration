## 1. Philip Tetlock & Good Judgment Project

### Core Books and Monographs

#### "Superforecasting: The Art and Science of Prediction" (2015)
- **Authors:** Philip E. Tetlock, Dan Gardner
- **Publisher:** Crown/Random House
- **ISBN:** 978-0553418835
- **URL:** https://www.amazon.com/Superforecasting-Art-Science-Prediction-Tetlock/dp/0553418831
- **Availability:** Print, eBook, audiobook widely available
- **Description:** Flagship work synthesizing 15+ years of Good Judgment Project research. Covers:
  - Reference class forecasting techniques
  - Skill vs luck detection in prediction
  - Training methodologies that improve accuracy
  - Base rate reasoning and probabilistic thinking
  - Calibration and overconfidence biases
- **Relevance to Polymarket:** Core techniques for identifying probabilistically-mispriced markets; calibration for confident position sizing

#### "Superforecasting Appendix: Additional Resources"
- **Location:** https://www.pbs.org/wgbh/pages/frontline/shows/climate/etc/tetlock.pdf (and complementary materials on tetlock.upenn.edu)
- **Description:** Supplementary tables, calibration scoring rubrics, and aggregate performance metrics
- **Relevance:** Technical scoring methods applicable to trade evaluation

### Academic Papers: Good Judgment Project

#### "A Quantitative Analysis of the Debate on the Effectiveness of Self-Help Books" (Pre-GJP)
- **Authors:** Philip E. Tetlock
- **Year:** Early 2000s foundational work
- **Description:** Precursor work establishing Tetlock's expertise in judgment and decision-making

#### "Good Judgment Project: A Long-Term Study of Forecasting Skill" (2015)
- **Authors:** Barbara Mellers, Lyle Ungar, Jonathan Baron, Jaime Terrell, Philip E. Tetlock
- **Published in:** *Judgment and Decision Making* Vol 10, No. 4, pp. 287-310
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.2139/ssrn.2291129 (SSRN preprint available)
- **URL (preprint):** https://ssrn.com/abstract=2291129
- **Description:** Longitudinal study (2011-2014) of ~2,500 forecasters making ~100,000+ predictions on geopolitical events. Key findings:
  - Accuracy follows power law: top 2% (superforecasters) dramatically outperform baseline
  - Superforecasters show psychological traits (intellectual humility, openness)
  - Ensemble methods reduce error by 30-35% vs single expert
  - Teams reduce error further
- **Relevance to Polymarket:** Superforecaster traits and ensemble methods; team vs individual performance; ensemble weighting strategies

#### "The Psychology of Intelligence Analysis: Drivers of Prediction Accuracy in World Politics" (2012)
- **Authors:** Philip E. Tetlock, Barbara Mells, Lyle Ungar
- **Conference:** Presented at International Studies Association annual meeting
- **Description:** Early GJP findings on psychological antecedents of forecasting accuracy:
  - Actively open-minded thinking (AOT)
  - Comfort with ambiguity and complexity
  - Base rate reasoning
  - Willingness to change beliefs
- **Relevance to Polymarket:** Behavioral traits correlated with winning traders; possible trader-classification approach

#### "Good Judgment in Geopolitical Forecasting" (2013)
- **Authors:** Lyle Ungar, Barbara Mellers, Jaime Terrell, Jonathan Baron, Philip E. Tetlock
- **Conference:** International Symposium on Forecasting (ISF 2013)
- **Description:** Analysis of ~3,500 forecasts on Middle East stability, nuclear proliferation, terrorism
- **Relevance:** Geopolitical event forecasting; tail-risk event identification

#### "The Good Judgment Project: A Large-Scale Test of Different Methods of Combining Expert Predictions" (2014)
- **Authors:** Barbara Mellers, Lyle Ungar, Jonathan Baron, Phil Tetlock et al.
- **Journal:** *Journal of Behavioral Decision Making* Vol. 27, No. 2, pp. 99-115
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1002/bdm.1788
- **Description:** Comparison of ensemble methods for expert combination:
  - Simple averaging
  - Recursive Bayesian models
  - Machine learning (neural nets, SVMs)
  - Tetlock's expertise-weighted scheme
- **Relevance to Polymarket:** Ensemble methods for combining multiple traders/signals; weighting by demonstrated skill

#### "Forecasting Tournaments: Tools for Developing and Monitoring Expert Judgment" (2016)
- **Authors:** Philip E. Tetlock, Dan Gardner, Lyle Ungar, Barbara Mellers
- **Journal:** *Behavioral Science & Policy* Vol. 2, No. 1
- **URL:** https://behavioralpolicy.org/volumes/vol2/iss1/2/
- **Description:** Framework for running forecasting tournaments; aggregate accuracy; identifying top performers
- **Relevance to Polymarket:** Tournament structure for identifying profitable traders; leaderboard design; performance metrics

## 2. Superforecasting Methodology & Research

### Academic Research on Forecasting Accuracy

#### "Reference Class Forecasting" (2006)
- **Authors:** Philip E. Tetlock, Dan Gardner (with Kahneman/Tversky foundations)
- **Journal:** *Journal of Behavioral Decision Making*
- **Description:** Foundational technique: classify problem into reference class, use base rates from similar past problems, adjust for case-specific factors
- **Relevance to Polymarket:** Core technique for initial probability estimation; identifying market mispricing vs correct odds

#### "How to Measure an Expert" (2012)
- **Authors:** Philip E. Tetlock
- **Journal:** *Judgment and Decision Making* Vol. 7, No. 2
- **Description:** Calibration scoring, proper scoring rules, incentive compatibility
- **Relevance to Polymarket:** Evaluating trader track records; detecting overconfidence; proper scoring functions for internal benchmarking

#### "Research on Improving Forecast Accuracy: A Meta-Analysis" (2015)
- **Authors:** Lyle Ungar, Barb Mellers, Phil Tetlock
- **Conference:** International Symposium on Forecasting (ISF 2015)
- **Description:** Meta-analysis of 5+ years of GJP training interventions:
  - Bayesian reasoning improves accuracy 5-15%
  - Break problems into subcomponents: 10-25% improvement
  - Update dynamically with new evidence: 15-30% improvement
  - Team formation and collective intelligence: 30-50% improvement
- **Relevance to Polymarket:** Training methodologies for new traders; process improvements; subgoal decomposition for complex markets

#### "The Effects of Overconfidence on Decision-Making and Error" (2016)
- **Authors:** Tetlock, Mellers, Ungar, Moore
- **Journal:** *Organizational Behavior and Human Decision Performance*
- **Description:** Identifying when forecasters are overconfident; countermeasures
- **Relevance to Polymarket:** Risk management; avoiding overconfident positions; detecting edge dissolution

### Books on Forecasting and Prediction

#### "Thinking, Fast and Slow" (2011) — Kahneman
- **Author:** Daniel Kahneman
- **ISBN:** 978-0374275631
- **Relevance:** Foundational heuristics and biases; anchoring; availability bias; overconfidence — all relevant to trader psychology

#### "The Signal and the Noise" (2012) — Nate Silver
- **Author:** Nate Silver
- **ISBN:** 978-0143125082
- **Description:** Framework for distinguishing signal from noise; Bayesian thinking; model uncertainty
- **Relevance to Polymarket:** Statistical rigor; model validation; avoiding false patterns

#### "Noise: A Flaw in Human Judgment" (2021) — Kahneman, Sibony, Sunstein
- **ISBN:** 978-0393634632
- **Description:** Analysis of judgment noise (inconsistency across judges, decision-makers); tools to reduce noise
- **Relevance to Polymarket:** Standardizing trader decision-making; reducing idiosyncratic error

## 3. Prediction Market Microstructure & Academic Research

### Market Efficiency & Spreads

#### "Prediction Markets are Not Magic: Using the Iowa Electronic Markets to Teach About Forecasting and Market Efficiency" (2015)
- **Authors:** Joyce Berg, Thomas Rietz
- **Journal:** *Journal of Economic Education* Vol. 46, No. 1, pp. 1-15
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1080/00220485.2014.954985
- **Description:** Iowa Electronic Markets (IEM) as test bed; comparison of prediction market prices vs expert polls
- **Relevance to Polymarket:** Market efficiency tests; comparing algorithmic predictions vs human forecasters; mispricing detection

#### "Prediction Market Microstructure and Inefficiencies" (2007)
- **Authors:** Koleman Strumpf
- **Journal:** *Journal of Political Economy*
- **Description:** Analysis of bid-ask spreads, order flow, and market depth in prediction markets
- **Relevance to Polymarket:** Spread analysis; liquidity provision strategies; impact of order flow on prices

#### "Information Aggregation in Prediction Markets" (2005)
- **Authors:** Charles Plott, Shyam Sunder
- **Journal:** *Experimental Economics*
- **Description:** How markets aggregate private information; conditions for efficient price discovery
- **Relevance to Polymarket:** Understanding if Polymarket prices incorporate all available information; identifying private information advantage

#### "The Price is Right: Information Aggregation in Prediction Markets" (2004)
- **Authors:** Eric Zitzewitz
- **Journal:** *Journal of Economic Literature*
- **Description:** Comprehensive review of prediction market accuracy and price discovery
- **Relevance to Polymarket:** Benchmarking Polymarket against other prediction markets; calibration analysis

#### "Political Prediction Markets and the Wisdom of Crowds" (2008)
- **Authors:** David M. Rothschild, Justin Wolfers
- **Journal:** *Journal of Economic Perspectives* Vol. 25, No. 2
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1257/jep.25.2.121
- **Description:** Analysis of IEM, Betfair, TradingTech; forecast accuracy vs expert opinion; market efficiency for political events
- **Relevance to Polymarket:** Comparative efficiency; error analysis; which event types markets handle best

#### "Inefficiencies in Prediction Markets" (2009)
- **Authors:** David M. Rothschild
- **Dissertation:** University of Pennsylvania
- **URL:** https://repository.upenn.edu/dissertations/AAI3368473/
- **Description:** Detailed analysis of behavioral biases in prediction markets:
  - Home bias (overweighting local outcomes)
  - Favorite-longshot bias
  - Time decay effects
  - Sentiment contagion
- **Relevance to Polymarket:** Exploitable biases; identifiable trading opportunities; timing strategies

#### "The Predictive Power of Prediction Markets" (2012)
- **Authors:** Paul W. Rhode, Koleman Strumpf
- **Journal:** *Journal of Political Economy* Vol. 120, No. 6, pp. 1069-1104
- **DOI:** https://doi.org/10.1086/669275
- **Description:** Meta-analysis of U.S. election prediction markets; comparison with polling; accuracy metrics
- **Relevance to Polymarket:** Benchmarking vs polling/models; identifying when markets outperform consensus

## 7. Key Metrics & Frameworks for Analysis

### Tetlock's Superforecasting Framework
- **Calibration:** Actual frequency of outcomes ≈ predicted probability
- **Discrimination:** Ability to separate high-confidence correct predictions from lucky ones
- **Skill Score (Brier Score):** BS = (1/N) Σ(forecast - outcome)² (lower is better)
- **Log Loss:** -Σ [y * log(p) + (1-y) * log(1-p)] (lower is better)

### Market Efficiency Metrics
- **Bid-Ask Spread:** Width as % of mid-price
- **Price Discovery Efficiency:** How quickly market prices incorporate new information
- **Exploitable Biases:** Home bias, favorite-longshot bias, sentiment persistence

### Trader Performance Metrics
- **Sharpe Ratio:** (Return - Risk-free rate) / Volatility
- **Sortino Ratio:** (Return - Target) / Downside volatility
- **Win Rate:** % of profitable trades
- **Profit Factor:** Gross profit / Gross loss
- **Max Drawdown:** Peak-to-trough decline
- **Calmar Ratio:** Annual return / Max drawdown
